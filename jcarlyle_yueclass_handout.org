* Preliminaries

** What is a Yuè Dialect?

A simple diagnostic [cite:@carlyle2020commonyue pp. 33-34]:

1. Presence of a phonemic lower /yīnrù/ (陰入) tone
2. A distinction between long, open \slash{}a\slash{} and short, central \slash{}ɐ\slash{}
3. The word for "slaughter" takes the general form of \slash{}tʰɔŋ¹\slash{} (劏)
4. The word for "thing" takes the general form of \slash{}ɲɛ⁴\slash{} (嘢)
5. The word for "noon" involves the general form \slash{}an⁵\slash{} (晏)
6. Presence of a feminine suffix for animals that takes the general form of \slash{}na³\slash{} (乸)
7. Presence of a 2nd person plural marker that takes the general form \slash{}ti⁶\slash{} (哋)
8. The word for "child" either takes the general form of \slash{}sɐj⁵ mɐn¹ tsɐj³\slash{} (細民仔) or \slash{}sɐj⁵ lɔw³ kɔ¹\slash{} (細佬哥)
9. The word for "(early) morning" involves some combination of the general forms /tʃiw¹/ (朝) and /tsɔw³/ (早)

Remarks:
- 5 or more -> (probably) Yuè Chinese

- Predicts S. Pinghua dialects are Yuè, /but/ N. Pinghua dialects are not.
